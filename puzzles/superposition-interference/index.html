Quantum objects exhibit a property called "Quantum Interference". When you let them interact with one another, their probabilities will change: some states might become more likely, other states might disappear completely.

This is analogous to water waves. When you throw two rocks at a lake, ripples are formed. You may notice that at some point, the ripples would cancel out each other. Similar things happen to quantum objects when they undergo quantum interference.

What is it useful for? If we have some superposition states that we want to avoid, we can try to remove these states before measurements. This allows us to get more favourable measurement, and is very useful in quantum computers.
