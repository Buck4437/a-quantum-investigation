This puzzle requires us to stabilise five crates using interference. We need to match the waveform of the stable state and the inversion of the unstable state.

There are five modes for the device, representing different kinds of waveforms:

  • 1: Sine
  • 2: Tangent
  • 3: Sawtooth
  • 4: Square
  • 5: Triangle

Note that there is a small margin for error. If the frequencies are close enough, then it would count as correct.

After solving the puzzle, we would obtain the answer COLLAPSE.

Afterwords: The wave mechanic is inspired by a mechanic in The Qubit Game. I want to introduce the concept of quantum interference, which works tightly with superposition. This leads to me using the constructive and destructive interferences of waves as an analogy of quantum interference. The musician analogy at the beginning also serves as an introduction to the amplifying or cancelling effect of quantum interference.

This puzzle tells us that Alice's and Bob's minds are entangled, suggesting some kind of relationship between them. If we press "Give Up", the puzzle will show the correct answer, which gives us information to deduce the relationship between Alice and Bob.

The relationships are as follows:

Level 1: Alice and Bob are thinking of the same thing.

Level 2: Alice's and Bob's number sums up to 100.

Level 3: Bob's letter is Alice's letter shifted 4 places (e.g. A -> E, B -> F... Z -> D).

Level 4: The first two bits of Alice's and Bob's bit string are equal. The last three bits of Alice's and Bob's bit string are flipped. (e.g. if Alice's bit string is "00000", then Bob's bit string is "00111").

After solving the puzzle, we would obtain the answer TOGETHER.

Afterwords: this analogy is used to introduce a basic concept of entanglement, that two objects can be related in some form. This also introduces the concept that if we know the definite state of one of the objects, then we can use the entanglement relationship to deduce the state of the other object.

In this puzzle, we need to deliver quantum balls using quantum teleportation. However, the analyser broke down and we would need to fix it.

The initial states of the two particles are 0/1 and A/B. We need to produce the specified output by using the given procedures. After experimentation, we can deduce what the procedures perform:

  • "Swap" swaps the two states of the quantum ball.
  • "Amplify" multiplies the two states of the quantum ball by 2.
  • "Sum" adds the second state of the quantum ball to the first state.
  • "Merge" generates all possible state combinations of the two particles. This information isn't needed to solve the puzzle.

Here are the procedures needed to calibrate each protocol:

Protocol 1: Swap, Amplify

Protocol 2: Sum, Sum, Swap

Protocol 3: Sum, Sum, Sum, Amplify

After solving the puzzle, we would unlock the last page of the puzzle. The terminal would display the measurement result, as well as a formatted string: "TRANSMISSION". This is the answer to this puzzle.

Afterwords: the presentation of this puzzle is quite different from others, being a half-story, half-puzzle hybrid. I want to introduce the concept of quantum teleportation, but I find that crafting a puzzle around it is very difficult. So, I settled on this alternative. Still, I hope that this won't distract the solver from understanding quantum teleportation.
